Postcards From Everywhere

A travel journal in postcards

Greetings!

High school isn’t all that bad as its been made out to be. Sometimes your swim coach confuses you for the other small Asian girl on the team, but thats okay! He’s old and he managed to mostly stop by the end of the season. In light of the recent Black Lives Matter movement, your mom asked me if you’ve experienced any instances of racism in the past. A fair question, since there have been multiple Black students who have come out with stories of discrimination and slurs thrown from teachers and students alike. I can’t think of anything, other than a few small comments here and there, can you? And its not like you’re that Asian anyways. Your mom has stopped you from bringing Korean food in the past and you shop at all the stores that the other girls do. Still, I’m sure it was a bit shocking when this popular White girl brought a salted seaweed snack (김 kim) to class and everyone Ooed and Aahed. Well, enjoy your first Homecoming dance! I’m sure it’ll be a lot of fun.

Greetings!

Aunt Lizzie’s place is so serene. It’s up a mountain and surrounded on all sides by trees. She has a couple of bunnies as well as a few cats roaming around. You would love it here. Your grandparents are staying with her for the time being. I know you’ve never met them, nor can you speak to them since you don’t know much Korean, but they’re so proud of you. Your grandfather (할아부지 hal-ra-bo-ji) is especially happy that you’re going to a Catholic university. Did you know that your father grew up Catholic? He also wants to know if you have a boyfriend yet, but especially if they’re Korean and religious. I know it's a dated question, but we all know that he was disappointed that all of your aunts married White men. Anyways, enjoy your senior year of high school!

Greetings!

South Korea is so beautiful. We took the subway all the way from Seoul to Chuncheon to visit your grandmother (할문이 hal-mon-ee). Your mother hasn’t seen her in person in over 30 years since she immigrated to America (미국 mi-gug) with her father and her siblings. Did you know that her parents are divorced? Come to think of it, you probably don’t even remember your grandfather (할아부지 hal-ra-bo-gee). Your grandmother seems like a cool person. She lives alone and runs her own tailoring business. She also doesn’t have a cell phone. Or email. Or the internet. Just a landline. And I know that even if you were to call her, the language barrier would still be an issue. When we visited, she had bought all this expensive fruit for us to eat. It was really nice of her! Hope you’re acclimating well to college!

Greetings!

It’s the annual Baek (백 pe-k) family reunion and we’ve all gathered in Georgia to celebrate your grandfather’s 80th birthday! Your dad prepared a slideshow of old photos and used AI to colorize them. Did you know that your grandfather escaped from North Korea when he was a kid? And that he was a 3-star military general in the South Korean army? It was only on the car ride to the Airbnb that your dad revealed that your grandparents worked in a mall selling wigs when they first settled in America. Did you know this? I know that you know that they lived in government housing growing up, but did you know what they really sacrificed so that you could grow up in America? Anyways, cheers and enjoy your senior year of college!
