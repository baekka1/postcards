Duly Noted

Postcards from an older sister I wish I had

Hey Katie!

How's your freshman year of high school going? Anyone been giving you trouble at school? It wasn't until I left high school and moved away from Texas that I realized how isolating it was to grow up there. Perhaps that is simply due to Southlake's suburban nature or it could be because you're probably one of a few Asian kids at the school. But its not like you’re that Asian anyways. Mom has stopped you from bringing Korean food in the past and you shop at all the stores that the other girls do. I was still able to have a good experience, and I hope that you will to! Just remember that the world is a lot bigger and there are more accepting places out there for you to discover, should you feel the need to. Have fun at the Homecoming dance!

Greetings!

Aunt Lizzie’s place is so serene. It’s up a mountain and surrounded on all sides by trees. She has a couple of bunnies as well as a few cats roaming around. You would love it here. Our grandparents are staying with her for the time being. I know you’ve never met them, nor can you speak to them since you don’t know much Korean, but they’re so proud of you. Your grandfather (할아부지 hal-ra-bo-ji) is especially happy that you’re going to a Catholic university. Did you know that your father grew up Catholic? I had no idea. I know growing up without a super strong connection to your family is tough, but just know that it can get better. Sometimes, you just have to make the first move. All the best for your last year of high school!

Greetings!

South Korea is so beautiful. We took the subway all the way from Seoul to Chuncheon to visit our grandmother (할문이 hal-mon-ee). Mom hasn’t seen her in person in over 30 years since she immigrated to America (미국 mi-gug) with her father and her siblings. Did you know that her parents are divorced? Our grandmother seems like a cool person. She lives alone and runs her own tailoring business. She also doesn’t have a cell phone. Or email. Or the internet. Just a landline. And I know that even if you were to call her, the language barrier would still be an issue. When we visited, she had bought all this expensive fruit for us to eat. It was really nice of her! Hope you’re acclimating well to college!

Greetings!

It’s the annual Baek (백 pe-k) family reunion and we’ve all gathered in Georgia to celebrate your grandfather’s 80th birthday! Dad prepared a slideshow of old photos and used AI to colorize them. Did you know that our grandfather escaped from North Korea when he was a kid? And that he was a 3-star military general in the South Korean army? It was only on the car ride to the Airbnb that dad revealed that your grandparents worked in a mall selling wigs when they first settled in America. Did you know this? I know that you know that they lived in government housing growing up, but did you know what they really sacrificed so that we could grow up in America? Anyways, cheers and enjoy your senior year of college!
